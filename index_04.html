Transforming African Higher Education Through Innovation

The Africa Higher Education Centres of Excellence (AAU-ACE) program has emerged as a transformative force in advancing higher education across the continent. Since its inception, the program has fostered collaboration between universities, research institutions, and industry partners to address Africa's most pressing challenges through innovative research and education.

Our centres have made significant strides in areas such as sustainable agriculture, renewable energy, public health, and digital transformation. Through strategic partnerships and collaborative research initiatives, we have successfully trained thousands of graduate students and researchers who are now leading change in their respective fields.

"The AAU-ACE program represents the future of African higher education - one that is research-driven, innovation-focused, and deeply connected to the needs of our communities."
- Prof. Olusola Oyewole, Secretary-General, Association of African Universities

Research Excellence and Impact

Over the past year, our centres have published over 500 peer-reviewed research papers, filed 45 patent applications, and established 30 new industry partnerships. These achievements demonstrate our commitment to generating knowledge that drives economic growth and social development across Africa.

The agricultural centres have developed drought-resistant crop varieties that are now being cultivated by farmers in 12 countries, while our health centres have made breakthrough discoveries in tropical disease research that are improving healthcare outcomes in rural communities. Meanwhile, our technology centres have created digital solutions that are revolutionizing education delivery and financial inclusion across the continent.

Building Sustainable Partnerships

Collaboration remains at the heart of our success. We have established partnerships with leading international universities, research institutions, and industry leaders to ensure that our research and training programs meet global standards while addressing local challenges. These partnerships provide our students and faculty with access to cutting-edge facilities, funding opportunities, and international networks.

Our industry partnerships have resulted in the creation of over 200 jobs for our graduates and have facilitated technology transfer that has benefited local communities. We continue to work closely with government agencies, NGOs, and private sector partners to ensure that our research findings are translated into practical solutions that make a real difference in people's lives.
